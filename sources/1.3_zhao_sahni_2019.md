# Zhao & Sahni (2019) — String Correction Using the Damerau-Levenshtein Distance

## Citation

> Zhao, L., & Sahni, S. (2019). String correction using the Damerau-Levenshtein distance. *Algorithms*, **12**(7), 136.
>
> **DOI:** [10.3390/a12070136](https://doi.org/10.3390/a12070136)

## Summary

This paper presents a comprehensive treatment of the Damerau-Levenshtein distance, which extends the classical Levenshtein distance to include **transpositions of adjacent characters** as a primitive edit operation. Zhao and Sahni analyze multiple algorithmic formulations for computing the Damerau-Levenshtein distance and propose corrections and optimizations.

**Key contributions:**

- A corrected and clarified formulation of the Damerau-Levenshtein algorithm, addressing known issues in earlier implementations (particularly the "adjacent transpositions" variant).
- Detailed complexity analysis of different DP-based approaches, including the O(nm) time and space formulation.
- Practical algorithms for string correction that minimize the number of Damerau-Levenshtein operations.
- Discussion of the relationship between the "optimal string alignment" distance and the true Damerau-Levenshtein distance.

## Relevance to Distance Compression Under Probabilistic Perturbation

The Damerau-Levenshtein distance is the **direct generalization** most relevant to distance compression under probabilistic perturbation, since transposition is one of the four canonical perturbation types. When modeling probabilistic perturbations over strings, including transpositions as a distinct operation changes the distance landscape significantly — two strings that are far apart under Levenshtein may be close under Damerau-Levenshtein. This means that compression schemes must account for the expanded operation space. Additionally, the distinction between "adjacent transpositions" and general transpositions affects the metric properties of the distance, which in turn affects how well compressed representations preserve metric structure. For perturbation models where transpositions occur with non-negligible probability, the Damerau-Levenshtein distance provides the natural framework for analysis.
